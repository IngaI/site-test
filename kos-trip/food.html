Eat Well
Best Restaurants — Steak, Local & Unforgettable
Lisa's Restaurant
Modern Mediterranean · Steakhouse
€€€
Consistently ranked Pristina's #1 upscale restaurant. Renowned for some of the finest steaks in the city — filet mignon with vegetables from €10, plus rib-eye and T-bone.
🥩 Must Order: Rib-eye steak + Kosovar wine from their curated list. Try the risotto as a starter.
Reservation: Call ahead, especially on weekends. Walk-in possible on weekday evenings.
Liburnia Restaurant
Traditional Kosovar · Since 1992
€€
The heart and soul of Pristina dining. Warm, rustic, beloved by locals and visitors alike. Traditional stews, grilled meats, and the signature Liburnia steak. Generous portions, low prices.
🥩 Must Order: Liburnia Steak, Kosovar meatballs, veal ribs, and warm bread. Also: chicken rice with sweet chili.
Tip: Go for lunch — even better value and less busy than evenings.
Hera Restaurant (Hotel Sirius)
European · Rooftop · 8th Floor
€€€
A hidden rooftop gem with stunning panoramic views of Pristina. European menu with strong steak selection and excellent service. The view alone is worth the visit at sunset.
🌇 Best for: Evening dinner with a view. The filet mignon gets strong reviews.
Meqa Qebaptore
Local Institution · Street Meat
€
One of Pristina's last surviving traditional qebaptore. Meat sold by the piece — koftas ~€0.30 each. Assemble your own mixed grill: qebapa, pleskavica (burgers), chicken livers, kidneys. Cash only. No frills. Pure experience.
🔥 Must Order: Mixed grill plate — order 8–10 pieces minimum. Eat with fresh flatbread and ajvar.
Country House Restaurant
Traditional · Hillside Garden
€€
Legendary hilltop restaurant outside Pristina — known as one of the best in Kosovo. Summer garden, sweeping views, outstanding traditional grills. Worth the taxi ride (€10).
🌿 Must Order: Roast lamb, stuffed peppers, grilled trout. Order house wine — local Kosovar red.
Paddy O'Brien's Irish Pub
Irish · Expat Favourite
€€
The social hub for British, American, and Scandinavian expats. Authentic Guinness (yes, really), traditional steaks and classic pub grub, sports TV. TripAdvisor Certificate of Excellence since 2013.
🍺 Best for: Post-work drinks and a reliable steak in a familiar atmosphere.
03
Eat Like a Local
Kosovo Local Specialities — Must-Try List
Dish What It Is Where to Find It Don't Miss
Flija
National Dish
 Layered crepe-style pastry cooked slowly over embers under a saç (metal lid). Rich, hearty, unique to Kosovo. Takes hours to make. Traditional restaurants; Prizren old town; rural villages Order with kos (strained yoghurt) and honey
Qebapa
Balkan Staple
 Grilled minced meat rolls (lamb/beef) served with flatbread, ajvar (red pepper relish), and onions. Kosovo's version is smaller and spicier. Meqa Qebaptore; any mehane; Prizren market stalls Order 6–8 pieces as a starter
Pleskavica
Kosovo Burger
 A large, flat spiced meat patty — halfway between a burger and a kebab. Often stuffed with cheese or peppers. Traditional grills and street food stalls Ask for it stuffed (punjena)
Suxhuk
Spiced Sausage
 Dried, highly spiced sausage — Ottoman legacy. Often served as a cold cut starter with cheese and bread. Any traditional restaurant; bazaar shops to take home Pair with aged Sharr cheese
Sharr Cheese
Regional
 A semi-hard, salty sheep's cheese from the Sharr Mountains. Like a Balkan pecorino. Kosovo's best-known artisan food product. Markets; upscale restaurants; mountain restaurants Buy a chunk at the market to take home
Burek
Ottoman Legacy
 Flaky phyllo pastry stuffed with meat, cheese, or spinach. Sold at burekgjia shops from 6am. The best breakfast in the Balkans. Street bakeries throughout Pristina — find any burekgjia Order mesë (meat) burek with kos on the side (€1–2)
Sarma
Winter Classic
 Stuffed cabbage rolls with minced meat and rice, slow-cooked. Deeply comforting — a Kosovo grandmother classic. Liburnia; Country House; traditional mehanë Best for a cool April evening
Rakia / Raki
National Drink
 Fruit brandy — plum (kumbullore) or grape. Raki e shtëpisë = house-made. Offered as a welcome drink almost everywhere rural. Traditional restaurants; mountain villages; the bazaar Never refuse a first glass — it's a cultural insult
Baklava
Ottoman Dessert
 Pristina and Prizren both have excellent baklava — lighter than Turkish versions, with walnut and honey. Sold by weight in sweet shops. Old bazaar in Prizren; sweet shops on Mother Teresa Blvd Buy 200g to eat on the street walk
Kosovar Macchiato
Coffee Culture
 Pristina runs on espresso. The "macchiato" here is an espresso with a dash of cold milk — not the Italian version. Ubiquitous and excellent. Every café in Pristina (try Soma Book Station or Espresso Lab) Never ask for a large coffee — embrace the tiny cup
