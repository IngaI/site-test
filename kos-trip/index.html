Quick Practical Tips
💶
Cash is King
Kosovo uses the Euro but card acceptance outside major hotels/restaurants is patchy. Always carry €50–100 cash. ATMs widespread in Pristina city centre.
🚕
Use eTaxi App
Pristina's best taxi app — tracked, metered, reliable. City centre rides rarely exceed €5. Always cheaper than flagging a cab. Download before you arrive.
🗣️
Language
Albanian is the language. Good English in the city centre, hotels, and younger generation. Learn: "Faleminderit" (thank you) and "Mirëmëngjesi" (good morning) — it goes a long way.
🍷
Kosovar Wine
Kosovo has a surprisingly good wine tradition — Vranac red and Chardonnay whites from the Rahovec region. A bottle in a restaurant costs €8–15. Take one home.
📱
SIM Card
Vala or Ipko SIM cards available at the airport and city shops — ~€5 for 5GB data. Kosovo is not in EU roaming zones, so avoid using your home SIM without checking costs.
🕌
Mosque Etiquette
Remove shoes, dress modestly, avoid visiting during the 5 daily prayer calls. All mosques welcome respectful tourists outside prayer times.
🥾
Packing for Hikes
For mountain hikes in late April: waterproof jacket, gloves, and warm mid-layer mandatory. Temperatures drop fast above 1,500m. Good grip boots essential.
☕
Coffee Culture
Pristina's café culture rivals Vienna's. Never rush coffee — it's a 45-minute social ritual. Ask for "kafe macchiato". Best cafés: Soma, Espresso Lab, Matisse.
